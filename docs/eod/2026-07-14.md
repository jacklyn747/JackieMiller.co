# EOD Report — July 14, 2026

**TL;DR:** We locked the site's positioning strategy — the market research validated the "AI for people whose work can't be wrong" thesis but killed "high-stakes" as the headline; the working direction is **"AI for work you sign your name to."** We found the anchor case study (the dismissed Dallas capital murder case) and designed the four-layer verification system that would have prevented it: Claude Skills as protocols, ledgers as state, triggers as cadence, MCP connectors as ingestion. Everything is written up in the project wiki and sitting in draft PR #1.

## Revelations

- The adoption gap Jackie identified is the best-documented finding in AI right now: ~90% of workers use AI, ~75% have abandoned tasks over untrustworthy output, and trust is *falling* as usage rises. The bottleneck is trust in output, not access.
- "High-stakes" is corporate wallpaper (Wolters Kluwer, FTI, Salesforce) and doesn't filter — everyone thinks their work is high stakes. Stakes must be defined by *consequence*, not importance.
- The Walker case failure was **entropy, not incompetence** — a competent person tracking too many stateful items with memory and a calendar. That failure shape is identical across legal, documentary, and the clinic pilot, which is what makes the skill library transferable IP.
- A Claude Skill is a *document the client can read* — the SKILL.md is the protocol, inspectable and versioned. For buyers whose objection is "I can't put my name on a black box," that's the sales argument made tangible.

## Decisions

- **Positioning:** keep the high-stakes strategy, kill the phrase as headline. Lead candidate: "AI for work you sign your name to." Backup: "For work that has to survive being checked." Rejected: "accuracy-first"/"verified AI" phrasing (reads like compliance software).
- **Promise checkable, never right.** No copy may guarantee correctness; the product is verification systems with human sign-off. (Liability + credibility.)
- **Architecture standard for client builds:** the four layers — skill = protocol, ledger = memory, trigger = discipline, connector = feed. See docs/system-architecture.md.
- **Proof format:** real failure → named cost → the system that would have made it impossible. The Walker case is the first write-up.
